Independent Commision Against Corruption Act, March 2024

Chapter 1: Scope

The purpose of the Independent Commission Against Corruption (ICAC) is to investigate, expose and prevent corruption involving or affecting public institutions and government officials, and to promote the integrity and accountability of public administration by creating an independent and accountable body.

Chapter 2: Jurisdiction

The Commission will be given the power to investigate any member of the Government of Discordia, any citizen who was formerly a member of Government or any member of a political organisation within Discordia.

Chapter 3: Corrupt Conduct

Corrupt conduct is any conduct of any Discordian citizen (whether or not a government official) that adversely affects, or that could adversely affect, either directly or indirectly, the honest or impartial exercise of official functions by the Government of Discordia. This could involve the following actions:

  • Abuse of power
  • Bribery
  • Blackmail
  • Branchstacking
  • Election fraud
  • Failure to report corrupt conduct to the Commission
  • Fraud
  • Intentionally misleading citizens
  • Perverting the course of justice
  • Revolving door tactics
  • Tampering of documents
  • Treason
  • Violation of legislation or the constitution in a public context
    • Chapter 4: Powers of ICAC

    The Commission will be given the power to require anyone under this definition to produce a statement of information. Failure to produce a statement shall be a criminal offence, unless it violates their constitutional rights under articles twenty-nine, thirty-one and thirty-two. The Commission will be able to summon witnesses and compel the production of evidence relating to Corrupt Conduct.

    Chapter 5: The Holding of Public Inquiries

    For the purposes of an investigation, the Commission will hold public inquiries for the purpose of investigating allegations of corrupt conduct within the jurisdiction of Discordia. Public inquiries shall be conducted with the objective of exposing to the public, and making it aware, of corrupt conduct, At a public inquiry, the person presiding must announce the general scope and purpose of the inquiry. The Commission may summon a person to appear before the Commission at a compulsory public inquiry.

    Public inquiries are to be chaired by each of the commissioners and a representative from each branch of government. Additionally, each political party may send a representative to be part of the chair if no one from their party already is, however it is not requisite for the inquiry to go fourth.

    Chapter 6: Prosecution

    The Commision will not have the power to sentence individuals, instead it will present evidence to the Court of Discordia which has the power to prosecute.

    Chapter 7: Organisational Structure

    The Commission will consist of a Chief Commissioner as well as at least two other Commissioners appointed by parliament with a two thirds majority. The Commissioners may not hold any other position of government or be a member of any political organisation.
