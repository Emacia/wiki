Absent Member Act, October 2023

In the event that a member remains absent for three consecutive voting sessions without formally declaring a leave of absence, they shall be issued an ultimatum. The ultimatum will establish the following options available to the member:

  • Voluntary resignation

  • Resumption of voting activity

  • Formal declaration of leave of absence

  • Continuation of non-participation in voting proceedings

Upon incurring an additional two consecutive absences subsequent to the issuance of the ultimatum, the parliament reserves the right to initiate a motion of no confidence against the aforementioned member which will require a two thirds majority. In cases where a member elects to declare a leave of absence, parliamentary approval must be obtained prior to the enactment of such leave.
