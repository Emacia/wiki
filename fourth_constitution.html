Constitution of the Republic of Discordia

Preamble

The Republic of Discordia is constituted as a federal republic of voluntarily united and equal peoples and a democratic community based on the powers of self-government to serve the common good. We, the Discordian people, commit ourselves to this Constitution:

Honoured of our national unity forged by Discordians from many nations;
But profoundly aware of the imperative need to further consolidate our unity;
Upholding freedom, tolerance, individual dignity and the rule of law;
Proudly affirm ourselves as bastion for republicanism;
And supportive of achievement as well as equality of opportunity for all.
With this profound conviction, we, as a republic, do solemnly and wholeheartedly pledge our unwavering allegiance to this Constitution, a document that encapsulates the very essence of our republic's identity and aspirations.

Section One: Government

Article One: The government shall be made up of three distinct branches:

  1. Legislative
  2. Executive
  3. Judicial

Article Two: The respective heads of these branches, individually elected by the populace, shall be:

  1. Prime Minister
  2. Premier
  3. Chief Justice

Article Three: The Chancellor shall serve as the presiding authority over these branches, collectively forming the "Presidium," the supreme organ of governance.

Article Four: The Presidium, composed of four members, should not be misconstrued as a legislative chamber.

Article Five: The line of succession from the Chancellor will be the aforementioned sequence from Article Two.

Article Six: Within the Presidium, if a vote is evenly split, the side of the Chancellor prevails.

Article Seven: The Chancellor does not automatically possess the prerogative to dismiss members of government.

Section Two: The Legislature

Article Eight: The Legislature shall be recognised as the "Discordian Parliament."

Article Nine: The size of Parliament shall be determined as a number between one tenth and one twentieth of the population. The Prime Minister will have the power to choose this number with the approval of parliament.

Article Ten: Should necessity arise, Parliament may be subdivided into multiple chambers or chambers may be amalgamated together, contingent upon unanimous consent from all Presidium members and an absolute majority of parliament members, cabinet members and court justices respectively.

Article Eleven: Parliament reserves the prerogative to initiate a Constructive Motion of No Confidence against any or all Presidium members. Upon invoking Article Eleven and securing the Prime Minister's consent, half of the parliamentary membership is requisite for ratifying the motion, whereas a two-thirds majority shall be required without the Prime Minister's consent. This action shall trigger a new election for the vacated role. In instances where a motion is brought against the Prime Minister, an absolute majority shall be able to ratify the motion regardless of how the Prime Minister votes.

Article Twelve: All government officials shall have the right to propose legislation to Parliament. All Discordians can request a law be put forth by a government official.

Article Thirteen: Parliament shall possess the power to amend and approve all legislation. An absolute majority of all members of parliament is needed to pass legislation into law. In no circumstance can a bill be passed into law without half of all member’s approval. The Presidium may vote to veto the law once passed. In such instances, a two thirds majority by Parliament shall be requisite to override the veto.

Article Fourteen: The leader of Parliament shall be the Prime Minister who will automatically gain membership of the Parliament’s highest chamber.

Section Three: The Executive

Article Fifteen: The executive power is vested in the Chancellor and exercisable by the Premier.

Article Sixteen: The Executive shall wield authority over matters pertaining to their respective ministry. Other organs of government should seek to aid the executive where possible.

Article Seventeen: Parliament shall retain the power to nullify Executive decisions. However, the Executive has recourse through Judicial Appeal.

Article Eighteen: Parliament and the Premier must approve of new ministries before they are created or removed.

Article Nineteen: The Premier shall have power to appoint and dismiss ministers.

Article Twenty: Ministers may not serve in any other organ of government.

Section Four: The Judiciary

Article Twenty-One: The Judiciary shall be vested with the power of Judicial Review, thereby empowered to declare Legislative or Executive enactment unconstitutional or unlawful.

Article Twenty-Two: The Judiciary shall comprise the Chief Justice and not less than two Justices appointed by the Chief Justice. The Chancellor's consent is requisite for these appointments.

Article Twenty-Three: The Chief Justice retains the prerogative to effect the removal of another Justice unilaterally. Concurrently, the Chancellor, Prime Minister, Premier and Parliament can effectuate the removal of a Justice, necessitating mutual approval.

Article Twenty-Four: When a court vote is evenly split, the side of the Chief Justice prevails.

Section Five: Moderation

Article Twenty-Five: Moderation is to be exercised by a group of moderators, duly appointed by a member of the executive that parliament designates responsible with a two-thirds majority vote in their favour. Anyone may nominate themself as a moderator.

Article Twenty-Six: Moderators shall not currently hold positions within the Legislative, Executive, or Judicial branches of the Republic of Discordia, ensuring their impartiality in upholding the tenets of our governance.

Article Twenty-Seven: Moderators shall be entrusted solely with the diligent enforcement of existing laws.

Section Six: Rights & Freedoms

Article Twenty-Eight: Equitable treatment before the law is guaranteed to all Discordians. All Discordians shall be protected from systemic discrimination and prejudice. No person shall be favoured or disfavoured by the government because of sex, race, language, ethnicity, culture, sexual orientation, gender identity, physical and mental disability or religious and political opinions in any circumstance within the server.

Article Twenty-Nine: Every Discordian shall be able to exercise the right to express their opinions, thoughts, and criticisms regarding the government, its officials and its policies. No individual exercising their right to criticise the government shall face undue legal repercussions, harassment, or intimidation. There shall be no unwarranted legal actions arising from the exercising of this right.

Article Thirty: All workers are guaranteed the right to strike, protest, withdraw their labour and to own the means of production. The government shall not unduly infringe upon the autonomy of workers to organise, assemble, and advocate for their interests. All workers shall have flexible working arrangements to achieve a work-life balance and to accommodate time zone differences.

Article Thirty-One: Those accused of breaking a rule or law shall be granted the right to a trial before their fate is decided. No Discordian shall be denied the right to counsel before and during their trial. Those indicted shall be informed promptly, in detail, of the nature and cause of the accusation against them and have adequate time and facilities for the preparation of their defence. Obtaining a confession or evidence by means of threat, enticement or deceit shall also be strictly prohibited. No Discordian shall be compelled in any criminal case to be a witness against themself. Everyone charged with a criminal offence shall be presumed innocent until proven guilty according to law. All defendants shall be reminded of this article before a court case can proceed.

Article Thirty-Two: The privacy and confidentiality of Discordians shall be respected and protected. No government entity shall engage in any action or conduct that infringes upon the personal matters of citizens.

Article Thirty-Three: Discordians have the right to elect and be elected in a transparent, inclusive, equitable, pluralistic and democratic selection and designation system that guarantees their participation. Discordians have the right to form political parties, movements and causes, recruit and campaign for them, join or withdraw from them and participate in all the decisions adopted by them.

Section Seven: Miscellaneous

Article Thirty-Four: An individual may contest up to two positions of public office concurrently and occupy only one position.

Article Thirty-Five: The conduct of elections shall be overseen by an impartial entity entirely dissociated from the government.

Article Thirty-Six: Disagreements between organs of government or between government and citizens that cannot be solved by court are to be mediated by the Chancellor until an agreement is met. If the Chancellor declares mediation unsuccessful, a binding referendum shall be held, requiring an absolute majority.

Article Thirty-Seven: Any law or action found in contravention of this constitution shall be rendered null and void.

Article Thirty-Eight: The electoral process shall adhere to the ensuing guidelines:

  • The Presidium will be elected via Instant-Runoff Voting.
  • Parliament will be elected via a system of proportional representation.

Article Thirty-Nine: The Flag of Discordia shall consist of three horizontal bands, wherein the uppermost band shall be adorned in red, the middle band in blue, and the lowermost band in black. Adjacent to the hoist side of the flag, there shall be a vertical blue pile, featuring a yellow sun with eight points centrally positioned within the pile.

Article Forty: The creation of official titles without approval from parliament is prohibited.

Article Forty-One: This constitution is a living document and thus alteration is allowed so long as it preserves the core principles and values enshrined herein. The alteration of this constitution necessitates a referendum that attains a three-fifths majority. There shall be two ways to initiate a referendum:

  • The unanimous endorsement from each presidium member.
  • The approval of three-quarters of parliament.

The referendum must be announced at least twenty-four hours prior to its commencement with all proposed amendments clearly outlined.

Section Eight: Constitutional Amendments

Amendment One: The Chief Justice's approval shall be required for any laws passed by parliament pertaining to courts.

Amendment Two: Although Discordians have the right to trial, this right does not extend to the most extreme of circumstances. Parliament shall have the power to define said circumstances. Only the presidium shall be given the power to enforce this.

Amendment Three: Parliament shall have power to make laws surrounding parliamentary vacancies. Filling a vacancy will not necessarily require an election and members may be selected via countback or appointment.

Amendment Four: Parliament shall have the power to create laws preventing those convicted of breaking law from holding a position of public office.

Amendment Five: The Prime Minister, Premier and Chief Justice shall have the power to appoint a deputy in their position. The position of deputy can assume the full powers of the member if their respective superior declares leave of absence. In the event of a presidium member going missing, the other presidium members may vote to allow the deputy to assume the full powers of their superior. In the event of the Chancellor declaring leave of absence or going missing, the presidium shall vote on decisions that would normally require the Chancellor. They shall have the power to unilaterally remove their respective appointed deputy.

Amendment Six: The government shall remain secular and shall refrain from holding official positions regarding religious, spiritual, mythological, supernatural or theological beliefs system or lack thereof. No organisation of the like shall receive any privileges from the government, nor exercise any political authority. No person shall be compelled to take part in any religious act, celebration, rite or practice. The right to personal views on these matters by individual citizens and members of government shall be protected as well as ideological influences from said matters.

Amendment Seven: Acts such as providing assistance to raids, exercising authority in a manner that jeopardises national security or Discordian sovereignty, failing to report a recognised threat to the establishment, or inciting insurrection shall be deemed treasonous offences. The consequence for engaging in these actions shall be the exile of the implicated citizen.

Amendment Eight: Freedom and privacy of correspondence of the citizens of Discordia shall be protected. No organisation or individual may, on any ground, infringe on citizens' freedom of privacy of correspondence, except in cases where to meet the needs of national security or criminal investigation.

Amendment Nine: No Court Justice shall judge in their own case or a case where they may have an interest.

Amendment Ten: The practice of slavery or involuntary servitude in any form shall not exist in Discordia. The government shall take appropriate measures to eradicate any instances of such practices.
